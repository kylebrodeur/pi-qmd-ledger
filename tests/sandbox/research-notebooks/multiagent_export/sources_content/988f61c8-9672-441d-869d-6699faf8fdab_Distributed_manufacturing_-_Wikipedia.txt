From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Coordinated decentralized manufacturing

Distributed manufacturing

, also known as 

distributed production

, 

cloud producing, distributed digital manufacturing,

 and 

local manufacturing

, is a form of decentralized manufacturing practiced by enterprises using a network of geographically dispersed manufacturing facilities that are coordinated using 

information technology

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_technology

. It can also refer to local manufacture via the historic 

cottage industry

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cottage_industry

 model, or manufacturing that takes place in the homes of consumers.

Enterprise

[ 

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&action=edit&section=1

]

In enterprise environments, the primary attribute of distributed manufacturing is the ability to create value at geographically dispersed locations. For example, 

shipping

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shipping

 costs could be minimized when products are built geographically close to their intended markets. 

[1]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-1

 Also, products manufactured in a number of small facilities distributed over a wide area can be customized with details adapted to individual or regional tastes. Manufacturing components in different physical locations and then managing the 

supply chain

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_chain

 to bring them together for final assembly of a product is also considered a form of distributed manufacturing. 

[2]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-Chrisman-2

 

[3]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-K%C3%BChnle2010-3

 Digital networks combined with additive manufacturing allow companies a decentralized and geographically independent distributed production ( 

cloud manufacturing

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_manufacturing

). 

[4]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-Bopp2010-4

Consumer

[ 

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&action=edit&section=2

]

Within the 

maker movement

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maker_movement

 and 

DIY

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DIY

 culture, small scale production by consumers often using 

peer-to-peer

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer-to-peer

 resources is being referred to as distributed manufacturing. Consumers download digital designs from an 

open design

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_design

 repository website like 

Youmagine

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Youmagine&action=edit&redlink=1

 or 

Thingiverse

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thingiverse

 and produce a product for low costs through a distributed network of 

3D printing

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_printing

 services such as 

3D Hubs

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_Hubs

, 

Geomiq

https://www.geomiq.com/

. In the most distributed form of distributed manufacturing the consumer becomes a 

prosumer

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prosumer

 and manufacturers products at home 

[5]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-5

 with an 

open-source 3-D printer

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_3-D_printer

 such as the 

RepRap

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RepRap

. 

[6]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-6

 

[7]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-7

 In 2013 a desktop 3-D printer could be economically justified as a personal product fabricator and the number of free and open hardware designs were growing exponentially. 

[8]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-8

 Today there are millions of 

open hardware

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_hardware

 product designs at hundreds of repositories 

[9]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-9

 and there is some evidence consumers are 3-D printing to save money. For example, 2017 case studies probed the quality of: (1) six common complex toys; (2) 

Lego blocks

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lego

; and (3) the customizability of open source 

board games

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Board_game

 and found that all filaments analyzed saved the prosumer over 75% of the cost of commercially available true alternative toys and over 90% for recyclebot filament. 

[10]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-:0-10

 Overall, these results indicate a single 3D printing repository, 

MyMiniFactory

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MyMiniFactory

, is saving consumers well over $60 million/year in offset purchases of only toys. 

[10]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-:0-10

 These 3-D printers can now be used to make sophisticated high-value products like 

scientific instruments

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_instrument

. 

[11]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-11

 

[12]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-12

 Similarly, a study in 2022 found that 81% of open source designs provided economic savings and the total savings for the 3D printing community is more than $35 million from downloading only the top 100 products at YouMagine. 

[13]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-13

 In general, the savings are largest when compared to conventional products when prosumers use recycled materials in ' 

distributed recycling and additive manufacturing

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distributed_recycling_and_additive_manufacturing&action=edit&redlink=1

' (DRAM). 

[14]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-14

Social change

[ 

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&action=edit&section=3

]

Some 

[15]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-triple-c.at-15

 

[16]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-16

 

[17]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-17

 call attention to the conjunction of 

commons-based peer production

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commons-based_peer_production

 with distributed manufacturing techniques. The self-reinforced fantasy of a system of eternal growth can be overcome with the development of economies of scope, and here, the civil society can play an important role contributing to the raising of the whole productive structure to a higher plateau of more sustainable and customised productivity. 

[15]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-triple-c.at-15

 Further, it is true that many issues, problems and threats rise due to the large 

democratization

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratization

 of the means of production, and especially regarding the physical ones. 

[15]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-triple-c.at-15

 For instance, the recyclability of advanced nanomaterials is still questioned; weapons manufacturing could become easier; not to mention the implications on counterfeiting 

[18]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-18

 and on " 

intellectual property

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_property

". 

[19]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-19

 It might be maintained that in contrast to the industrial paradigm whose competitive dynamics were about 

economies of scale

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economies_of_scale

, commons-based peer production and distributed manufacturing could develop economies of scope. While the advantages of scale rest on cheap global transportation, the economies of scope share infrastructure costs (intangible and tangible productive resources), taking advantage of the capabilities of the fabrication tools. 

[15]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-triple-c.at-15

 And following 

Neil Gershenfeld

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neil_Gershenfeld

 

[20]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-20

 in that "some of the least developed parts of the world need some of the most advanced technologies", commons-based peer production and distributed manufacturing may offer the necessary tools for thinking globally but act locally in response to certain problems and needs. As well as supporting individual personal manufacturing 

[21]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-21

 social and economic benefits are expected to result from the development of local production economies. In particular, the humanitarian and development sector are becoming increasingly interested in how distributed manufacturing can overcome the supply chain challenges of last mile distribution. 

[22]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-22

 Further, distributed manufacturing has been proposed as a key element in the 

Cosmopolitan localism

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmopolitan_localism

 or cosmolocalism framework to reconfigure production by prioritizing socio-ecological well-being over corporate profits, over-production and excess consumption. 

[23]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-23

Technology

[ 

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&action=edit&section=4

]

By localizing manufacturing, distributed manufacturing may enable a balance between two opposite extreme qualities in technology development: 

Low technology

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_technology

 and 

High tech

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_tech

. 

[24]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-auto-24

 This balance is understood as an inclusive middle, a "mid-tech", that may go beyond the two polarities, incorporating them into a higher synthesis. Thus, in such an approach, low-tech and high-tech stop being mutually exclusive. They instead become a dialectic totality. Mid-tech may be abbreviated to "both…and…" instead of "neither…nor…". Mid-tech combines the efficiency and versatility of digital/automated technology with low-tech's potential for autonomy and resilience. 

[24]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-auto-24

Contracting in Distributed Manufacturing

[ 

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&action=edit&section=5

]

Distributed manufacturing (DM) is a production model that decentralizes manufacturing processes, enabling products to be designed, produced, and distributed closer to end-users. This shift from centralized production to localized networks offers advantages such as increased flexibility, cost efficiency, and local empowerment. However, it also introduces significant challenges in contracting due to the decentralized nature of roles and varying stakeholder responsibilities.

Research into contracting and order processing models tailored for distributed manufacturing has highlighted the need for flexible, role-based frameworks and advanced digital tools. 

[25]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_manufacturing#cite_note-25

 These tools and frameworks are essential for addressing issues related to 

quality assurance

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quality_assurance

, payment structures, legal compliance, and coordination among multiple actors. By addressing these challenges, contracting models for distributed manufacturing can unlock its potential for more localized, efficient, and sustainable production systems. A 

system prototype

https://distributed-orders.fly.dev/

 has been developed to simplify contracting for distributed manufacturing. This tool allows buyers to manage orders across multiple manufacturers using a single interface, automating workflows to ensure clarity and accountability for everyone involved. This research was led by the 

Internet of Production

https://www.internetofproduction.org/

, as part of the 

mAkE

https://makeafricaeu.org/

 project (African European Maker Innovation Ecosystem), funded by the European Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme.
