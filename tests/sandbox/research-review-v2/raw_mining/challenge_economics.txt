│ networking, and contracting [3]. Because DLT enables secure, distributed     │
│ exchanges without the need for a trusted third-party intermediary, the       │
│ technological "overhead" of verification is minimized, making decentralized  │
│ economic coordination highly viable [3, 4].                                  │
│                                                                              │
│ **The Value of Tracking Material Provenance**                                │
│ Rather than viewing the tracking of materials as a prohibitive overhead, the │
│ sources frame it as a necessary mechanism to solve the environmental and     │
│ social damage caused by opaque, centralized supply chains [5]. Organizations │
│ are actively deploying DLT to create transparent material provenance without │
│ exorbitant friction:                                                         │
│ *   **Provenance** uses DLT to track the attributes, authenticity, and       │
│ journey of materials and ingredients in consumer products to allow for       │
│ positive purchasing choices [5].                                             │
│ *   **Skuchain** uses RFID tags and barcodes tied to DLT to digitally enable │
│ the secure transfer of goods across the global economy [5].                  │
│ *   **Foodtrax** tracks food from origin to shelf to eliminate waste         │
│ resulting from improper handling and storage [5].                            │
│                                                                              │
│ **Centralized Suppliers vs. Decentralized "Cosmo-Local" Production**         │
│ The research contrasts the traditional "global factory" model—where parts    │

│ knowledge and design are shared globally, but physical manufacturing and     │
│ material sourcing are localized as close to the point of human need as       │
│ sensibly possible [6, 8].                                                    │
│                                                                              │
│ The systemic benefits of this decentralized material model include:          │
│ *   **Replacing Economies of Scale with Economies of Scope:** Decentralized  │
